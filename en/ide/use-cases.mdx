Here is a summary of the changes each role experiences after adopting CodePilot.

## From the Developer's Perspective

> **"Repetitive busywork is gone, and I can focus solely on design and logic."**

* **Before**: Analyze requirements → search the web → piece together code → fix errors (repeat)
* **After**: Hand off requirements → AI generates a draft → review and refine the core logic


## From the Team Lead's Perspective

> **"Code review time has dropped, and the quality of the whole team's output has leveled up across the board."**

* **Before**: Spending a lot of time correcting junior developers' code style and pointing out basic mistakes
* **After**: The IDE applies team rules (Lint/Format) up front before submission, so reviews focus only on logic


## From the Executive / Operations Perspective

> **"We control the risks of adopting AI and confirm the productivity gains with data."**

* **Before**: No visibility into who uses which AI, concerns about data leakage, and unpredictable costs
* **After**: Control models and permissions through CodePilot Admin, and optimize costs with usage monitoring
